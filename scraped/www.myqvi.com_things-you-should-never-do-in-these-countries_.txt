7 Normal Things You Should Never Do When Visiting These Countries 
Ibraheem
July 17, 2026

Nobody is expecting travellers to know every local custom. But learning one or two before arriving can save an awkward moment and make the experience feel more respectful. Something completely normal at home, from leaving a tip to eating on public transport, may mean something very different elsewhere. These little cultural surprises are part of what makes travelling interesting. So, before packing the bags, here are 7 everyday things you should avoid in these countries.

1. Japan: Don’t Automatically Leave a Tip
Credit: Giphy

Tipping may feel polite elsewhere, but it is generally not expected in Japan. In the limited situations where a tip may be appropriate, such as with some private guides, Japan’s tourism board recommends offering it discreetly rather than leaving loose cash on the table.

2. Italy: Don’t Order a Cappuccino After Your Meal
Credit: Giphy

Cappuccino is traditionally treated as a morning or breakfast drink. After lunch or dinner, an espresso is the more typical choice, which means that giant cup of foam could announce “tourist” before the first sip.

3. France: Don’t Skip “Bonjour”
Credit: Giphy

Walking into a shop, café or bakery and immediately asking a question can come across as abrupt. France’s official tourism platform advises visitors to begin with “Bonjour” or “Bonsoir,” and simple phrases such as “s’il vous plaît” and “merci” also go a long way.

4. Thailand: Don’t Point Your Feet at People or Sacred Objects
Credit: Giphy

In Thai culture, feet are traditionally viewed as the lowest and least clean part of the body. Travellers should avoid pointing their soles towards people, Buddha images or religious objects, particularly when sitting inside temples.

5. Iceland: Don’t Enter the Pool Without Showering First
Credit: Giphy

Before entering an Icelandic swimming pool or geothermal bathing facility, visitors are expected to shower thoroughly without their swimwear. It is a hygiene rule rather than an optional spa suggestion, although some facilities provide private cubicles.

6. The UAE: Don’t Photograph Strangers Without Permission
Credit: Giphy

That perfect street photograph may need to wait. The UAE government advises visitors not to photograph people without their permission, and certain government, military and transport-related locations may also be restricted.

7. Singapore: Don’t Turn Public Transport Into a Dining Room
Credit: Giphy

Eating and drinking are prohibited on Singapore’s public buses, and travellers are expected to keep open food and drink containers properly sealed. The rule helps keep the famously clean transport system exactly that way.
